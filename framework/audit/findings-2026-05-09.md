## 1. Executive verdict

This extension run completes Phase 2 over the 46 citations queued by the 2026-05-08 rodage. Combined with the rodage, **all 76 distinct citations from the inventory now have a Phase 2 verdict**.

The extension confirms the framework's evidentiary base is broadly sound: 33 of 46 newly-verified citations earned `VERIFIED` (well-known historical facts, foundational textbooks, established standards, and current code-metric tools). However, **three new HIGH-severity findings** surfaced in the same pattern as the rodage's F-01 (Miller) — load-bearing **attributions to the wrong author or methodology**:

- **F-18 (Schein → Argyris):** "Espoused vs in-use values" attributed to Schein but originates with Chris Argyris.
- **F-19 (RUP spike → XP):** Spike pattern attributed to RUP but originates with Extreme Programming.
- **F-20 (Lean Startup "scope as variable" → Agile/PRINCE2):** "Scope is the variable" attributed to Lean Startup but is canonically Agile (Iron Triangle) or PRINCE2 (tolerance) framing.

In all three cases the *concept* the framework borrows is real and useful; only the source attribution is wrong. None of these affect the framework's structural design (phases, councils, retrofit handling), but together they constitute the same class of citation-precision issue the rodage already flagged for Miller, CMMI, and ISA 320 — mis-attribution within an attribution-heavy borrowed-vs-novel table.

Two additional MEDIUM findings concern (a) Linux CoC "grandfathering" claim lacking explicit primary-source documentation, and (b) M&A IT due-diligence "1 PW per 10–50 KLOC" rule of thumb that is plausible practitioner heuristic but unsourced — a tier-floor-rule violation per IVP v0.2 § 4.1. Six LOW findings concern edition-currency hygiene (PMBOK, PRINCE2), publication-year off-by-one (Putnam SLIM 1991 vs 1992), loose attribution scope (Spinellis), and minor framework-text imprecision (kaizen "5 days" vs documented 3–5; ARB "> 2 services" threshold).

The framework's two foundational policies — 40k-as-naming-only and industry-standards-over-invention — continue to be honoured at the document-text level. None of the new findings is framework-invalidating; all are citation-precision remediation candidates.

#### F-18 — Schein/Argyris attribution mix

- **Where:** `framework/phases/phase-0-for-debate.md:178` ("Borrowed from organizational ethnography (Edgar Schein, *Organizational Culture and Leadership* — espoused vs in-use values)"). Load-bearing for Phase 0's borrowed-vs-novel attribution table (C-035).
