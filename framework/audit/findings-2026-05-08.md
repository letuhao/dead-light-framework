## 1. Executive verdict

Within the Phase 2 citations sampled in this rodage, the framework's anchoring on real, citable industry standards is mostly holding — the policy "industry standards over framework-invented formulas" is not empty; most spot-checked citations resolve to genuine sources. However, **three HIGH-severity findings** exist where a load-bearing citation either contradicts its source (Miller's number), cites a superseded edition (CMMI v2.0), or attributes to a standard a number that the standard does not actually mandate (ISA 320 5–10%). Several MEDIUM findings concern attribution drift between standards and practitioner conventions (PMBOK SPI/CPI thresholds, ITIL Sev tiers, Spolsky's "correlate strongly"). The framework's two foundational policies — 40k-as-naming-only and industry-standards-over-invention — appear to be honoured at the document-text level, with one self-correction explicitly recorded in debate 001's Methodological Note.

The HIGH findings are remediation candidates, not framework-invalidating issues. None of them touch the framework's structural design (phases, councils, retrofit handling); they touch citation precision in a calibration-supporting catalogue.

## 4. Findings (severity-binned)

### CRITICAL

*None in this rodage pass.*

### HIGH

#### F-01 — Miller's number contradicted

- **Where:** `framework/debates/001-laws-count-and-multirepo-scaling.md:32` ("Miller's working-memory range, ~5 ± 2"). Load-bearing for the "≤ 5 Immutable Laws" cap rationale.
- **Source's actual claim:** Miller (1956), "The Magical Number **Seven**, Plus or Minus Two." Average limit ~7 items, range 5–9.
- **Framework's claim:** "~5 ± 2" attributed to Miller.
- **Why this matters:** Either (a) the citation should say 7±2 (Miller's actual number) and the cap rationale must accept that 5 is *below* the lower bound, or (b) the citation should be Cowan (2001, *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*), "The magical number 4 in short-term memory" — proposing ~4 ± 1, much closer to Dead Light's "5". Right now the framework cites the wrong author for its number.
- **Recommended action:** `obvious fix`: replace citation with Cowan 2001 (4 ± 1) and adjust the prose; the "≤ 5" cap then sits correctly above Cowan's lower bound. Alternative: keep Miller and weaken the rationale ("at the lower edge of Miller's working-memory range").

#### F-02 — CMMI v2.0 superseded

- **Where:** `framework/calibration-standards.md:20, 119, 122, 156`. CMMI is invoked as the anchor for CAR, PPB, QPM in Phase 0 calibration.
- **Source's actual state:** CMMI V3.0 released April 2023 by CMMI Institute (now under ISACA). V2.x retired June 2024; only V3.0 appraisals accepted from January 1, 2024.
- **Framework's claim:** "CMMI for Development v2.0 (2018)" cited as current.
- **Why this matters:** Edition currency is part of the "industry-standards-over-framework-invented" policy. Citing a retired edition undercuts the policy. The relevant practice areas (CAR, PPB, QPM) likely persist into V3.0 but the framework should confirm and update.
- **Recommended action:** `defer to project owner`: confirm which CMMI v3.0 practice areas correspond to v2.0's CAR/PPB/QPM and update citations. References list at line 156 also requires update.

#### F-03 — ISA 320 5–10% misattributed

- **Where:** `framework/calibration-standards.md:18` and inherited references in `pm-calibration-guide.md` and `phase-0-for-debate.md`. Load-bearing for materiality threshold reasoning.
- **Source's actual claim:** ISA 320 (IAASB) does not specify "5–10% of revenue/assets/expenses" as a materiality benchmark. ISA 320.A8 gives examples: 5% of profit before tax (profit-oriented manufacturing), 1% of total income/expenses (not-for-profit). The standard emphasizes professional judgement; preparers tune to context. Practitioner methodology documents commonly use ranges like 3–10% of PBT or 0.5–2% of revenue.
- **Framework's claim:** "Quantitative: typically 5–10% of relevant base (revenue, assets, expenses)" attributed to "ISA 320 / GAAP / IFRS materiality."
- **Why this matters:** The 5–10% figure is real practitioner heuristic but is not what ISA 320 says. The mismatch between the framework's text and the standard is exactly the kind of detail an academic reviewer would flag.
- **Recommended action:** `obvious fix`: re-phrase as "Practitioner heuristic: typically 5–10% of profit before tax (per ISA 320.A8 example range), or 0.5–2% of revenue. ISA 320 itself prescribes professional judgement; the percentage range is firm-methodology convention." The framework's adaptation to "% of project budget / KLOC / effort" can stay if anchored on the heuristic, not on the standard's text.

- **Recommended action:** `obvious fix`: re-phrase as "Spolsky (2000) argues from the Netscape case that big-bang rewrites are the single worst strategic mistake a software company can make."

#### F-07 — AWS LP "criticized inside Amazon" qualifier

## 8. Limitations of this rodage

1. **Single-pass single-reviewer.** Inter-rater reliability uncalculable. Repeat by a different agent or human reviewer would strengthen findings.
2. **Disconfirming-search coverage incomplete.** Run on ~6 of 30 verified items; should be run on all load-bearing items in a full pass.
3. **No primary-source PDFs read in full.** Verification rested on T2 summaries and well-known web sources. For HIGH findings (F-01 to F-03), reading the actual paper or standard text would harden the verdict but was not done in this pass to keep the rodage tractable.
4. **Industry-pragmatic mode admits T3.** Grey literature was used to triangulate (e.g., AWS Executive Insights for two-pizza). A stricter pass that admitted only T1/T2 would not have verified some items.
5. **Search-engine recency bias.** May have missed older or non-English foundational sources.
6. **AI-agent reviewer.** Web search effectiveness bounds what is found. Some sources behind paywalls (e.g., direct ISA 320 PDF without IFAC login) were not opened.
7. **46 of 76 citations not individually verified.** Listed in § 7. The aggregate stats in § 2 must be read with this denominator caveat.
8. **Phase 1 inventory was extracted by the same agent that wrote the framework's audit spec in the same session.** Independence is therefore reduced; an outside reviewer running IVP fresh would ideally also redo the inventory.
9. **`framework/audit/independent-verification-pass-for-debate.md` was excluded from scope** as a deliberate self-reference defence; auditing the audit doc with itself in the same pass is improper. Defer.
